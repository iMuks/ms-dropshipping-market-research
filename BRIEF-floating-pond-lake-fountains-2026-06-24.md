**Persona:** Two cores — (a) the rural/acreage stock-pond owner (1/3–1.5 acre, price- and electricity-conscious, DIY-prone) and (b) the hobby koi/water-garden keeper (emotionally invested in fish + noise-sensitive near the house). Higher-AOV tier = estate owners, HOA & golf-course managers. They sharply distinguish **"fountain" (decorative/surface)** from **"bottom/diffused aeration" (functional)** — load-bearing vocabulary. They distrust cheap pumps and return runarounds; trust is built by a visibly-honored long warranty, decades-long lifespan proof, and human pre-sale guidance.

**Recommended positioning angle:** *"The quiet, fish-safe, never-replace-it pond aerating fountain — backed by a warranty we actually honor and humans who size it for you."* Lead with durability (counter the "dies in 6–12 months/junk" 1-star pattern with 20-yr + honored-5-yr-warranty proof), quiet operation (ship/curate genuinely quiet units), and real oxygenation (speak the "bottom aeration vs decorative fountain" language). Decorative spray + low electric cost as secondary. Replicate the winning retailers' consultative "we'll size it for your acreage" support + prominent warranty.

**Willingness-to-pay:** Two-tier — sub-$200 DIY floor (utility pump "$100"; compressor "$60") vs the **$1,500–$3,000+ "real fountain" tier** (Kasco VFX "Starting at $1,762.00," 4.9/5 across 284+ reviews — yourpondpros.com). High-ticket play lives in the upper tier.

**What NOT to do:** (1) Don't sell a decorative surface fountain as whole-pond fish aeration — community warns of fish kills despite surface-only treatment (playitkoi.com/pages/aeration-vs-fountains). (2) Don't ignore noise/placement near property lines ("driving the neighbours mad"). (3) Don't trust cheap pumps for continuous duty.
